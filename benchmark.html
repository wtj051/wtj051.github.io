TrustEnergyBench

A public benchmark for trustworthy AI agents and physics-guided foundation-model components in critical energy systems.

TrustEnergyBench evaluates whether AI systems for energy forecasting, emergency control, storage dispatch, and data-center energy coordination can remain accurate, physically feasible, robust, uncertainty-aware, and life-aware under realistic stress conditions.

Why this benchmark?

Purely data-driven energy-AI models can produce outputs that violate power-flow equations, voltage/security limits, battery SOC/SOH boundaries, degradation-risk budgets, or grid-service constraints when operating conditions shift. Meanwhile, AI-driven data centers introduce fast-growing and increasingly flexible computing demand that should be modeled jointly with storage, renewable integration, cooling flexibility, and grid constraints rather than treated as fixed load. TrustEnergyBench is designed to make these failure modes and flexibility opportunities measurable, reproducible, and comparable.

Initial benchmark tracks

Track Task Evaluation focus
SafeFlex Dynamic storage flexibility and safe grid operation under contingencies, renewable uncertainty, and EV charging demand. Power-flow feasibility, voltage/security violations, contingency robustness, cost/flexibility tradeoffs.
BatteryLife Battery future-capacity, SOH/RUL, uncertainty, and degradation-aware dispatch features. SOH/RUL error, uncertainty calibration, degradation-risk violation, dispatch value, lifetime impact.
DataCenterFlex Coordinated planning and operation of flexible AI-driven data-center demand, energy storage, renewable supply, and grid interaction under uncertainty. Peak-load reduction, energy cost, renewable utilization, service-quality constraints, storage degradation, grid-stress mitigation, and robustness to demand/price/renewable uncertainty.

DataCenterFlex: evaluation design

Inputs

  • Data-center workload and demand scenarios
  • Renewable generation and electricity price uncertainty
  • On-site or grid-edge battery constraints
  • Cooling/thermal flexibility and service-quality limits
  • Distribution-grid or transmission-interface stress signals

Outputs

  • Workload-shifting or demand-flexibility decisions
  • Battery charge/discharge schedules
  • Grid-support or peak-shaving response
  • Uncertainty-aware risk scores

Metrics

  • Total energy and demand cost
  • Peak-load and grid-stress reduction
  • Renewable utilization and carbon-aware operation
  • Service-quality violation rate
  • Battery degradation and lifetime impact

Planned release sequence

  • v0.1: static task definition, sample data, evaluation script, baseline table, and documentation.
  • v0.2: public/synthetic grid-battery-data-center scenarios, public battery-data pipeline, and first reproducible leaderboard.
  • v1.0: stable benchmark version with versioned datasets, baselines, submission protocol, citation, and governance policy.

Repository

The benchmark repository will contain code, sample data, documentation, issue templates, contribution guidelines, citation metadata, and GitHub Actions workflows for validating submissions.
