Research

My research develops trustworthy, physics-guided AI for power systems, battery intelligence, energy storage, data-center energy systems, and grid-battery-computing coordination. The central question is:

How can AI make fast decisions for clean-energy infrastructure while respecting physical feasibility, device health, flexible computing demand, privacy, uncertainty, and operational accountability?

Theme 1: Physics-trustworthy AI for safe grid operation

I develop AI methods that embed power-system constraints into learning and control models, including power balance, power-flow solvability, voltage/security limits, transient stability, dispatch rules, and expert knowledge.

Representative directions:

  • Graph/time-series reinforcement learning for power-flow solvability restoration.
  • Transfer reinforcement learning for security-constrained redispatch across heterogeneous grids.
  • Topology-aware and knowledge-informed emergency-control models.
  • Physics-based safety metrics for AI agents and energy foundation models.

Theme 2: Life-aware battery intelligence

Battery health estimates become operationally useful when they are translated into safe capacity, power boundaries, efficiency, degradation cost, and dispatch constraints. My work connects SOH/SOC/RUL/future-capacity estimation with energy-storage operation and grid-edge decision support.

Representative directions:

  • Physics-guided deep learning for future capacity and remaining useful life prediction.
  • Continuous-time degradation modeling under irregular sampling and missing data.
  • Weakly supervised SOH estimation under imprecise intervals.
  • Health-to-dispatch battery toolboxes for lifecycle-aware storage flexibility in grid and data-center contexts.

Theme 3: Data-center energy intelligence

Rapid growth in AI computing is turning data centers into major, flexible, and uncertainty-sensitive energy assets. My emerging research studies how data-center demand, on-site storage, renewable supply, cooling flexibility, and grid constraints can be jointly modeled and coordinated without treating computing load as an exogenous fixed demand.

Scientific question. How can data centers become grid-aware flexible resources while maintaining computing service quality, reducing peak demand, improving renewable utilization, and respecting battery lifetime and operational constraints?

Representative directions:

  • Endogenous modeling of AI-driven data-center demand and workload flexibility.
  • Learning-based uncertainty quantification for computing load, renewable output, prices, and grid stress.
  • Coordinated planning of energy storage and flexible data-center demand under uncertainty.
  • Multi-timescale coordination across workload scheduling, battery dispatch, cooling/thermal flexibility, and grid-support decisions.
  • System-level evaluation of cost, peak-load reduction, renewable integration, battery degradation, and operational risk.

Future lab vision: Trustworthy Energy Intelligence Lab

My future lab will develop physics-guided AI, energy foundation models, and trustworthy decision systems that enable power grids, batteries, electric vehicles, data centers, and distributed storage to operate safely, sustainably, and grid-responsively under uncertainty.
